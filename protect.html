Why Protect a PDF file?

PDF documents often contain highly sensitive information like financial statements, legal contracts, or trade secrets. By adding password protection, you ensure that only individuals with the correct password can open, view, or modify the document's contents. This is an essential step for data security and compliance.

How to Password Protect a PDF

  • Upload: Provide the PDF document you wish to lock by dropping it in the area above.
  • Set Password: Type a strong password containing a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Confirm it to avoid typos.
  • Encrypt: Click "Encrypt PDF". ToolsPDF will process the encryption to secure your document.
  • Download: Save the newly generated, password-protected PDF to your device.

Benefits of Offline Security

When you use cloud-based services to protect a document, you ironically expose your unencrypted file to a remote server before it gets encrypted. ToolsPDF solves this by executing the encryption logic directly inside your browser. Your sensitive files never leave your device.
